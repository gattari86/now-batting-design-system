01 / Philosophy

Clean, athletic, built for sunlight.

Now Batting runs outside, in direct sun, on the dugout bench, in the dust. Every pixel earns its place. Readability over aesthetics. Speed over complexity. Neon is a flourish, never a wallpaper.

01

Sunlight first

High contrast text, thicker weights, no delicate grays on white for anything load bearing.

02

Moment driven

Neon highlights the active player, the waveform, the one thing happening right now. Nothing else.

03

Fast by default

One tap to play. Generation flow held under five steps. Taps feel immediate, confirmations feel human.

04

Low cognitive load

Clean athletic energy, not gaming chaos. One primary action per screen, no stacked CTAs, no decorative noise.

02 / Color System

Sunlight first, neon only for moments.

Warm off-white base, disciplined text ramp, two accents doing two very different jobs. Click any swatch to copy the value. Neon is emotional. Daylight is functional. Never confuse them.

Rule 01
Never put neon on pure white for text.
Contrast fails outdoors and fails accessibility. For any text that must be read, put neon on dark.
Rule 02
Daylight for buttons. Neon for moments.
Daylight holds up in direct sun and reads as a CTA. Neon is reserved for the single thing happening right now.
Rule 03
State is never color alone.
Active rows get a neon bar, tinted background, and bolder weight. Hierarchy still works in glare or for color-blind users.

Hierarchy rules

Every text decision answers one question.

  • Player names bold. Song titles medium. Metadata muted gray. The hierarchy IS the UI.
  • Minimum body 16px. Do not shrink type to fit data. If it does not fit, cut the data.
  • H1 is for moments, not every screen. One hero per flow. Step down everywhere else.
  • Mono is a garnish, not a voice. Labels, specs, numbered steps. Never body copy.
