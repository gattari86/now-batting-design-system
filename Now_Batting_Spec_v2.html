01 / Philosophy

Clean, athletic, built for sunlight.

Now Batting runs outside in direct sun, on the dugout bench, in the dust. Every pixel earns its place. Readability over aesthetics. Speed over complexity. Neon is a flourish, never a wallpaper.

Sunlight first
High contrast text, thicker weights, no delicate grays on white for anything load bearing.
Moment driven
Neon highlights the active player, the waveform, the one thing happening right now. Nothing else.
Fast by default
One tap to play. Generation flow held under five steps. Taps feel immediate, confirmations feel human.
Low cognitive load
Clean athletic energy, not gaming chaos. One primary action per screen, no stacked CTAs, no decorative noise.
North Star

Build a super clean, sunlight-readable app where neon is used sparingly to highlight the moment, not the whole UI.

07 / Interaction

Motion and feedback

Tap response
scale(0.98) on press. Transitions 150-250ms. Feedback feels physical, not rubbery.
Generation state
Clean loading with an optional waveform pulse in neon. No spinners, no skeletons, no filler copy.
Plain error language
"Couldn't reach the music service. Try again." No error codes, no jargon, no shame spirals.

09 / Outdoor readability

Sun test checklist

Can I read this in one second, in direct sun?
No low-contrast text anywhere a user needs to act.
No light gray on white for key info (player name, song title, time).
Avoid thin weights. Minimum 500 on anything labeled.
Avoid tiny icons. 20pt minimum on active controls.
Daylight accent (B8CC00) for buttons that must survive noon.
State never communicated by color alone. Pair with shape, weight, or position.
10 / One-line Direction

Build a super clean, sunlight-readable app where neon is used // sparingly // to highlight the moment, not the whole UI.
